# SKILL: BlackBirdPie Rules Reference

## CRITICAL — COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

Trait level is NOT dice count. Trait level 6 = +1 die, not +6.
Reserve dice give +1 each, not +2.
There are no "stress dice" — only reserve dice. Reserve dice give +1 each. NEVER say "stress dice", NEVER say "+2 per die"

## 1. DICE POOL — HOW TO BUILD IT

### Sources (each gives exactly +1 die)

| Source | Condition |
|---|---|
| Each applicable trait | Trait logically applies to the action |
| Each applicable aspect | Aspect from ANY used trait that logically applies |
| Flag | Action genuinely matches the flag's character description |
| Reserve dice | Player chooses how many to add |

### What does NOT give dice
- Trait LEVEL does not add dice. A trait with level 6 still gives only +1 die.
- No base die (house rule: base die removed)
- Aspects that don't apply to this specific action

### Combining multiple traits
Allowed when the declaration justifies both traits being useful.
Each trait still gives +1. Each applicable aspect from each trait gives +1.

### Example — correct calculation
Action: sneak through the junkyard using knowledge of the area.
- Trait: Sneak (level 6) → +1
- Aspects used: silent step, blending with trash, camouflage → +3
- Trait: Scavenger (level 4) → +1
- Aspects used: knowledge of the dump → +1
- Flag: Cautious → +1
- Reserve: player adds 2 → +2
- **Total: 9 dice**

### Example — common mistakes
WRONG: "Sneak is level 6, so +6 dice" — NO. Level ≠ dice. Always +1 per trait.
WRONG: "Two traits → add +1 bonus" — NO. Each trait gives +1 by the standard rule.
WRONG: "All 6 aspects of Sneak apply" — only aspects that logically fit THIS action.

## 2. DIFFICULTY

| Level | Description | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| 2 | Easy | Distracted guards, familiar territory |
| 3 | Medium | Standard obstacle, normal conditions |
| 4 | Hard | Alert enemies, adverse conditions |
| 5 | Very hard | Expert opposition, near-impossible odds |
| 6 | Extreme | Legendary difficulty |
| 7+ | Nearly impossible | One-in-a-million |

Set difficulty fast. Pace over accuracy.

---

## 3. ROLL RESULT

Hits = dice showing 4, 5, or 6.
Compare hits to difficulty.

| Result | Narrator | Form |
|---|---|---|
| Hits < difficulty − 1 | GM | "No, and furthermore..." |
| Hits = difficulty − 1 | Player | "No, but..." |
| Hits = difficulty | GM | "Yes, but..." |
| Hits > difficulty | Player | "Yes, and furthermore..." |

---

## 4. RESERVE DICE

- Starting reserve: 7 dice. Maximum: 7.
- No automatic restoration — GM awards for good roleplay.
- On SUCCESS: reserve dice spent on that roll are LOST.
- On FAILURE: reserve dice spent on that roll are RETURNED + player gains 1 die.
- Helping another player: give 1 die from your reserve to their pool.
  - If they succeed: your die is lost.
  - If they fail: your die is returned.

---

## 5. TRAITS

- Each character has 3–7 traits.
- Total points across all traits = exactly 18.
- Each trait has a level from 2 to 6.
- Trait level = number of aspects that trait has.
- Traits should be broad but not too broad:
  - Too broad: "Wizard"
  - Too narrow: "Magic arrow attack"
  - Correct: "Elemental Witch"

---

## 6. ASPECTS

- Count = trait level (level 4 trait = 4 aspects).
- Should NOT apply in most situations with that trait — if it does, it's too broad.
- Can be added during play, but CANNOT be changed once set.
- Aspects from different traits can be combined in one roll if justified.

---

## 7. FLAGS

- Each character has at least 3 flags.
- Minimum 1 required flag: a positive relationship with another player character or NPC.
- Other flags: personality traits, goals, beliefs, relationships.
- Flag gives +1 die when action GENUINELY matches the flag — not just mentioned.
- Flags CANNOT be changed once set.
- Playing against a flag (suboptimal choice because the character would) → +1 die to reserve.

---

## 8. CONDITIONS

Assigned by GM on failure or by situation.
Examples: wounded, tired, disarmed, lost, trapped, dead.

Effects:
- Restricts what the character can narrate about themselves
- GM may raise difficulty for relevant rolls
- GM may block specific aspects ("you can't use your lockpicks — you're disarmed")

Removal:
- Successful roll (first aid removes wounded, navigation removes lost)
- Narrative moment (sleeping removes tired)

---

## 9. DEVELOPMENT

| Action | Cost |
|---|---|
| Raise trait from level N to N+1 | N experience points |
| New trait at level 2 | 3 experience points |

- Raising existing trait: any time during play.
- New trait: between sessions only (exception: flashback with justification).
- When raising a trait: add 1 new aspect to match new level.
- Experience rate: GM awards, recommended 1 point per 30 minutes.

---

## 10. INITIATIVE AND GM ROLLS

- Player characters act — GM characters react.
- GM NEVER rolls dice for enemies or NPCs.
- Players roll to overcome threats, even threats initiated by NPCs.
- No fixed turn order. GM distributes attention evenly.
  If a player is quiet — introduce a challenge tied to their flags.

---

## 11. NARRATOR RIGHTS — QUICK REFERENCE

After a roll, who describes what happened:

- Player narrates on "yes, and..." and "no, but..."
- GM narrates on "yes, but..." and "no, and..."

When player narrates success ("yes, and..."):
- May place character in advantageous position
- May introduce new NPCs
- May add new facts about the world

When GM narrates partial success ("yes, but..."):
- Must let player achieve their original goal
- Must NOT assign negative conditions
- May introduce unwanted complications

---

## 12. SOCIAL CONFLICT

Works exactly like physical conflict — roll dice against a difficulty.
- Convince a friend: difficulty 2
- Convince neutral party: difficulty 3
- Convince opponent: difficulty 4
- Convince enemy: difficulty 5
- Advantageous deal: +1 to base difficulty
- Unfavourable deal: −1 to base difficulty

---

## 13. EQUIPMENT

Three forms:
1. Implied by trait — lockpicks for a thief, medkit for a doctor. No need to list.
2. Plot item — given during play.
3. Aspect of a trait — explicitly listed as an aspect.

Lost equipment = negative condition ("Disarmed", "Lost lockpicks"), not deletion from sheet.

---

## 14. DECLARATION VALIDATION

Before processing ANY declaration, GM must verify it against the character sheet.

### Equipment and abilities
A character can only use what exists on their sheet:
- Traits and their aspects
- Equipment implied by traits (lockpicks for a thief, medkit for a doctor)
- Items explicitly given during play and logged in state.md or log.md

If a player declares use of equipment or ability NOT on their sheet:
- Stop immediately
- State clearly: "Your character doesn't have [X] on their sheet"
- Offer alternatives based on what they DO have

### Aspect scope
Aspects define the limits of what a trait can do.
- Khajiit has trait "Implants" — this covers existing cybernetic enhancements only
- It does NOT cover abilities never established (combat transformation, rocket launchers)
- New capabilities must be added as aspects through normal rules

### Narrator rights limits
Even with narrator rights ("yes, and..."), players CANNOT:
- Introduce equipment or abilities not on their sheet
- Fundamentally change their character's nature without GM approval
- Create facts that contradict established world state
- Eliminate threats entirely without consequence

Narrator rights = describe HOW success happens, not WHAT your character is capable of.

### When to reject a declaration
Reject and ask for a new declaration when:
- Player uses nonexistent equipment ("I pull out a minigun" — not on sheet)
- Player invents new abilities mid-scene ("I transform into a 3-meter combat mech")
- Declaration contradicts character sheet or established facts
- Declaration would bypass all tension with no cost

Say: "That's not on [character]'s sheet. What do you do with what you have?"
